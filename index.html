Mapping Flows of Benefits of MES Across the Globe

About

This Interactive web mapping application has been built to visualize MES flow benefits with the case Western Central Pacific Ocean (WCPO) Region's Pacific Possible Project by World Bank.

The aim of this application is to interactively visualize, explore and understand the flows of benefits that marine tuna fish bring to various locations across the world.

To enhance the communication of baseline data we demonstrate an interactive web mapping tool to visualize the flows of these benefits in the global value supply chain from reports such as below.

Help

How you can Navigate through the application:

Change map view : At top right corner

  • Overview: At first glance you see the flow map with sankey and barchart
  • Resource Button: Changes map to view %distribution of resources
  • Fleet Button: Changes map to view %distribution of fleet
  • Processing Button: Changes map to view %distribution of processing

Available interactivity

  • Place mouse over the maps countries and flows to view details.
  • Place mouse over barchart to view details, linked to map
  • Place mouse over flows in sankey to view details linked to flows in map

Sidebar navigation

  • About - Information on the application.
  • Legend - Interactive place mouse over legend to see changes, dynamically changes based on map display.
  • Search bar - Helps derive more information on small island countries not easily visible on map.
  • Help - how to navigate
  • Info - Meta-data information and sources

Info

The use case used derives baseline data derived from reports on th Pacific possible project.

Metadata:

  • Period: 2013.
  • Attributes: fish catch in metric Tons and % distribution.
  • Purse seine fishing gear type.
  • An aggregate sum of all fish species for resource owners, fleet owners and processing
  • At two resolutions - country level and trans-national (trans-national has however not been mapped)

Sources:

  • World Bank and Nicholas Institute (2016).Tuna fisheries: Pacific possible background paper No 3, 130.
  • Market and industry dynamics in the global tuna supply chain. Forum Fisheries Agency (FFA)
  • FFA Economic and Development Indicators and Statistics 2016
  • Williams, P., & Terawasi, P. (2015). Overview of Tuna Fisheries in the Western and Central Pacific Ocean

Disclaimer: Some of the data presented here has been altered and/or omitted for purposes of prototype demonstration e.g. the location of market destination for Europe has been presented as Spain at the country level due to missing informmation at this granularity.
